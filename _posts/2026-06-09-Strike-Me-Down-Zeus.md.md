I think Zeus is punishing me.

In a moment of classical inspiration, I set out to reread *"The Iliad"*. God be with Junior Cert Classics.

In the summer air, with time and the weather on my side, the war had begun. I breezed through the first dozen or so books. Swift-footed Achilles withdrawn. Diomedes, Hector, Ajax. Each of them knowing the fury of the gods.

But life always catches up. Football, work, friends. *"The Iliad"* fell to the wayside. In all honesty, its slow pace and methodical descriptions of the battles did not launch me back into reading it. 

Now, here is where it gets interesting. In a dramatic turn of events, the weather in the past week, has been abysmal. Rain, hail, thunder, lightning. Trademark Zeus. He who drives the storm-cloud. Lord of the lightning flash.

I mean, put the pieces together. I am not a superstituous guy, but maybe I should pick it up again. Just to be safe. 

Rage and death await me. I'll choose the version on the pages though.
